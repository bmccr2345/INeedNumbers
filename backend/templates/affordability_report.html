Home Affordability Terms - Explained Simply

PITI (Principal, Interest, Taxes, Insurance)
Your complete monthly housing payment including loan payment, property taxes, and homeowner's insurance. This is what you'll actually pay each month.
Debt-to-Income Ratio (DTI)
The percentage of your gross monthly income that goes to all debt payments (including your new mortgage). Lenders typically prefer DTI under 36% for best rates.
Loan-to-Value Ratio (LTV)
The percentage of the home's value you're borrowing. LTV above 80% typically requires Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI) for additional lender protection.
Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI)
Insurance that protects the lender if you default on your loan. Required when down payment is less than 20%. Can be removed once you reach 20% equity.
Principal & Interest
Principal pays down your loan balance. Interest is the cost of borrowing money. Together, they make up your base mortgage payment before taxes and insurance.
Property Taxes
Annual taxes on your property value, typically paid monthly through your mortgage payment into an escrow account. Rates vary significantly by location.
