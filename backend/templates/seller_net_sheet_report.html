Seller Net Sheet Terms - Explained Simply

Sale Price
The agreed-upon purchase price for your home. This is the gross amount the buyer will pay before any deductions or adjustments.
Real Estate Commission
The fee paid to real estate agents involved in the transaction, typically 5-6% of the sale price. This is usually split between the listing agent and buyer's agent.
Seller Concessions
Credits or payments you agree to provide to the buyer to help with their closing costs, repairs, or other expenses. These reduce your net proceeds dollar-for-dollar.
Title/Escrow Fee
Fees paid to the title company or escrow agent for handling the closing process, title search, title insurance, and ensuring a smooth transfer of ownership.
Transfer Tax & Recording Fees
Government fees for transferring ownership of the property and recording the new deed. These vary by location but are typically a few hundred to a few thousand dollars.
Doc Stamps
Documentary stamp taxes required in some states on real estate transactions. The rate varies by state and is usually based on the sale price or loan amount.
Mortgage Payoffs
The remaining balance on your current mortgage(s) that must be paid in full at closing. This includes principal balance plus any accrued interest and fees.
Prorated Taxes
Property taxes are typically paid in advance. If you've prepaid taxes beyond the closing date, you'll receive a credit. If taxes are owed, you'll pay your portion up to the closing date.
💡 Important Considerations for Sellers
📋 Get Accurate Payoff Quotes
Contact your lender 10-15 days before closing for precise payoff amounts. Payoffs change daily due to interest accrual and may include prepayment penalties.
🏠 Factor in Repairs & Improvements
Budget for any agreed-upon repairs or improvements before closing. Home inspection findings often lead to additional costs not included in this estimate.
💰 Plan for Moving Expenses
Don't forget costs like moving companies, storage, utility transfers, and temporary housing. These can range from $2,000 to $10,000+ depending on your situation.
📊 Understand Market Conditions
In a buyer's market, you might pay more concessions or have higher marketing costs. In a seller's market, you might save on some expenses but pay higher agent fees due to competition.
🎯 Shop Around for Services
Title companies, attorneys, and other service providers may have different fee structures. Ask your agent for recommendations and compare costs when possible.
📅 Timing Your Sale
Closing date affects prorated taxes, utilities, and HOA fees. Strategic timing can save money, especially if you're moving to a more/less expensive area.
🏡 Capital Gains Considerations
If this is your primary residence, you may qualify for capital gains tax exclusions ($250K single/$500K married). Consult a tax professional about your specific situation.
🔍 Review All Estimates
This is an estimate only. Actual costs may vary based on final negotiations, inspection findings, appraisal results, and local market conditions.
⚠️ Items That May Increase Your Costs
• Buyer requests for repairs after inspection • HOA transfer fees or assessments • Outstanding liens or judgments • Property tax appeals or adjustments • Attorney fees for complex issues • Storage costs if closing is delayed
✅ Ways to Maximize Your Net Proceeds
• Price competitively for quick sale • Complete obvious repairs before listing • Negotiate commission rates and services • Shop for title/escrow services • Time closing to minimize prorated costs • Consider cash offers to avoid financing delays • Prepare all documents in advance
