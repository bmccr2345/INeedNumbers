Real Estate Commission Terms - Explained Simply

GCI (Gross Commission Income)
The total commission paid by the seller before any splits or deductions. This is divided between listing and selling sides, typically 50/50, though it can vary based on the agreement.
Commission Split
The percentage of your gross commission that you keep vs. what goes to your broker. Common splits range from 50/50 for new agents to 90/10 or higher for experienced agents with high volume.
Transaction Fees
Fixed fees charged by brokerages per transaction to cover administrative costs, marketing, technology, and support services. These are separate from commission splits.
Referral Fees
Fees paid when you receive a client through a referral from another agent or service. Typically ranges from 20-35% of your gross commission on that transaction.
Team Splits
If you're part of a real estate team, this is the percentage that goes to the team leader or shared team expenses in exchange for leads, support, and brand recognition.
Franchise/Royalty Fees
Fees paid to national franchise brands (like RE/MAX, Century 21, Coldwell Banker) for use of their brand, marketing materials, and support systems.
💡 Best Practices & Important Considerations for Real Estate Professionals
💰 Understand Your True Take-Home
Always calculate your net commission after all splits and fees. This is your actual income per transaction - use this for budgeting and goal setting, not the gross commission.
📊 Track Commission Trends
Monitor your effective commission rate over time. If it's declining due to increased fees or splits, consider negotiating better terms or changing brokerages.
🎯 Volume Negotiations
Higher transaction volumes often qualify you for better splits. Track your annual GCI and negotiate improved terms based on your production level.
💡 Value vs. Cost Analysis
Evaluate what you receive for your broker fees and splits. Higher splits aren't always better if you lose valuable support, leads, or marketing resources.
📝 Budget for Taxes
Set aside 25-30% of your take-home for taxes since you're likely an independent contractor. Consider quarterly tax payments to avoid penalties.
🏢 Compare Brokerage Models
Traditional brokerages offer more support but higher fees. Discount brokerages offer higher splits but fewer services. Choose based on your experience level and needs.
📈 Plan for Growth
As your business grows, renegotiate terms annually. Successful agents often move to higher split arrangements or even start their own brokerages.
🔍 Read the Fine Print
Understand all fee structures, including desk fees, monthly charges, marketing costs, and any caps on commission splits before signing with a brokerage.
⚠️ Red Flags to Watch For
• Brokerages requiring high upfront fees • Split structures that worsen with success • Hidden fees not disclosed upfront • No clear path to better terms with increased production • Contracts that are difficult to terminate
✅ Signs of a Good Brokerage Deal
• Transparent fee structure • Opportunity to improve splits with performance • Quality training and support • Strong brand recognition • Fair and clear contract terms • Technology and marketing support included
